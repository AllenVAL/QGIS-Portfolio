# QGIS Mapping Portfolio

This repository contains a collection of maps created using **QGIS**.  
The maps explore different geographic questions including population distribution, environmental exposure, climate change, public health, and voting accessibility.  

The goal of this portfolio is to demonstrate **cartographic design, spatial analysis, and geographic data visualization skills**.

---

# Maps

## U.S Population Dot Density Map

![U.S Population Dot Density Map](img/Lab2%20Map%201.png)

This map visualizes the distribution of population across the United States using a **dot density map**.  
Each dot represents a fixed number of people, showing where population clusters are concentrated.  
The map highlights major population regions such as the East Coast, California, and the Midwest.

---

## U.S Population Proportional Symbol Map

![U.S Population Proportional Symbol Map](img/Lab2%20Map%202.png)

This map combines **proportional symbols** with a choropleth background to represent state-level population.  
The size of the red circles indicates total population in each state, while the color of each state shows population ranges.

---

## SNAP Households by County in Washington

![SNAP Households by County](img/lab%203%20map%201.png)

This map displays the number of households enrolled in **SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program)** by county in Washington State.  
Darker red areas represent counties with higher numbers of households relying on food assistance.

---

## SNAP Participation Rate by Census Tract

![SNAP Participation Rate](img/lab3%20map2.png)

This map shows the **percentage of households using SNAP benefits** at the census tract level.  
A quantile classification scheme was used to evenly distribute values across categories and improve comparison across regions.

---

## Global CO2 Emissions and Current Emissions Distribution

![CO2 Emissions Map](img/lab%204%20map%201.png)

This map compares **cumulative CO2 emissions** with **recent emission locations**.  
A choropleth layer represents historical cumulative emissions, while red dots illustrate locations of current emissions.

---

## Mean Annual Exposure to Particulate Matter

![Particulate Matter Map](img/lab%204%20map%202.png)

This map visualizes **mean annual exposure to particulate matter (PM)** across the world.  
Higher exposure levels appear in darker red colors, particularly in regions with high population density and industrial activity.

---

## CO2 Emissions and Estimated GDP

![CO2 and GDP Map](img/lab4%20map3.png)

This map explores the relationship between **cumulative CO2 emissions** and **estimated GDP by country**.  
Larger symbols represent higher cumulative emissions while darker colors indicate higher GDP levels.

---

## Climate Change Precipitation Projection

![Precipitation Change Map](img/Lab%205%20Map%201.png)

This map shows projected changes in **annual precipitation across the United States** by comparing historical averages (1975–2005) with projected averages (2071–2090).  
Red areas indicate projected decreases in precipitation while blue areas indicate increases.

---

## Voting Accessibility and Drive-to-Vote Areas

![Drive-to-Vote Map](img/lab7%20map%201.png)

This map identifies areas where voters must **drive to reach ballot dropboxes**.  
Red areas represent regions outside the five-mile distance from dropboxes, while blue points represent eligible minority voters.

---

## Distance to Ballot Dropboxes

![Dropbox Distance Map](img/lab%207%20map%202.png)

This map uses **buffer analysis** to visualize areas within 1 to 5 miles of ballot dropboxes.  
The map highlights which parts of the county have easier access to voting infrastructure.

---

## Lead Exposure and Poor Health Outcomes in King County

![Health and Lead Exposure Map](img/Geog%20360%20Final.png)

This map examines the relationship between **lead exposure levels and poor health outcomes** at the census tract level in King County.  
By mapping both variables and identifying their intersections, the analysis suggests that areas with higher environmental exposure may also experience worse health outcomes.
